XS Board digital logic lab usage questions

Q:

We are using the XS Boards in our logic design laboratories. We have set up our lab PCs as follows:

  1. The parallel port on the motherboard (Chaintech VLM) is kept at its default value of address 378, IRQ 7. Mode changed to EPP (SPP and ECP w/ DMA also a possibility). The port is recognized under Win95 as LPT1.
  2. A "Side 2601" ECP / EPP parallel port ISA bus host adapter has been added. Address 278, IRQ 7, Mode EPP with no DMA. The port is recognized under Win95 as LPT2. As far as I understand SPP are not selectable on this board.
  3. Under Win95 a HP IIIP laserprinter has been added as a network printer that includes support for DOS programs. Printing takes place through (logical, not physical) printer port LPT3 equivalent to \\DIAET1\LASER1 on the printerserver placed on the same machine as the server itself (Novell 3.11). Printing functions are OK.

Is the selection of EPP OK?

A:

If XSLOAD and XSPORT work, then it is OK.

Q:

Is there any argument for a particular mating of the two parallel ports to the XS40 + XS95 combination, or are you free to choose?

A:

I assume you will connect the XS40 to one LPT and the XS95 to the other LPT. There should be no effect from choosing one or the other. You may want to create a batch file so the students don't have to type in the -p option every time since they are not on LPT1.

Q:

The ports both use IRQ 7. Is that a problem? Are the hardware / software using IRQ's at all?

A:

No, XSPORT and XSLOAD don't use interrupts.

Q:

Do you see any conflict between the speed of the EPP port and the cabel / XS-bord combination. The manual folowing the "Side 2601" board warns against reflections, crosstalk, lack of shielding / braid / twisted pairs / non- controlled impedence. Your experience?

A:

We haven't seen these problems. The edge rates on the standard printer ports are rather slow. I'm not sure about the characteristics of the Side 2601 drivers.

Q:

The boards will be powered by external regulated 5.1 V power supplies. The 5-volt on board regulators will have their input- and output-terminals connected with a short piece of wire. OK?

A:

You will probably be OK on this, but I would test it on one to make sure before doing the others. Why use 5.1V. Why not use the 9VDC power supplies.

Q:

Also on the XS40 boards (ver. 1.1) used with a 4000XL we use the 3.3 V regulator. We suppose that the regulator should run OK from the 5.1 vols supplied taking into account the relatively low current needed by the FPGA. OK?

A:

Yes, that is correct.

Q:

I have been greatly concerned with ESD in this lab setup. All equipment will get connected to the same earth. Our floor normally don't generate static electricity problems. Maybe unlucky combinations of floor / shoes / dress will. What is your experience with the XS boards?

A:

We have seen a few blown devices, but we don't know what the cause was.
