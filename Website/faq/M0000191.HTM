XS Board shows "9" during XSTEST

Q:

I am running the XSTEST to test my XS40 board V1.3 with Xilinx XC4010XL FPGA. There is only short display of number 9 for 2 seconds and then nothing appears, instead of displaying "O". I am using the Windows 95, but my computer does not have the option of SPP in the BIOS setup for parallel port mode.

A:

With pre-V3.0 XSTOOLs software, your board should display a "6" while the RAM is being loaded during XSTEST. You may have your board rotated by 180 degrees. But the fact that the LED is displaying something shows your board is properly configured for loading the RAM. That means the FPGA is probably OK.

Most blank displays are caused by the microcontroller executing incorrectly as a result of too high a clock frequency or a misplaced jumper. Try setting the programmable oscillator for a lower frequency such as 12.5 MHz. (It normally arrives set for 50 MHz.) Also make sure there is a shunt on the lower two pins of jumper J10 so the microcontroller will fetch instructions from the RAM.
