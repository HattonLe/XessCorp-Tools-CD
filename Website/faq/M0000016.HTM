Problems compiling a 512-location FIFO

Q:

I am using the FIFO_40.BIT example, as described in "The Practical Xilinx Designer Lab Book" as a basis for my own FIFO. As suggested in the "projects" section (page 251), I am trying to increase the size of the FIFO from 16 to 32768 by changing the read/write pointers etc to 15-bits etc. in the abel code. When I do synthesis>check syntax the check is successful, but once I do synthesis>synthesize, my PC takes forever to complete the task. I have successfully increased the size of the FIFO to 256 (using an 8-bit address bus) and my PC takes approx. 3 minutes to synthesize. If I go to a size of 512, my PC does not complete the synthesis in 30 minutes. (I have not left the program running to see how long it actually takes.) The PC i'm using is a P150 with 16MB of RAM. Has anyone come across this problem? or am I doing somthing wrong?

A:

Try placing the command "@CARRY 1;" into the EQUATIONS section of your ABEL code. This will make the logic synthesizer use a cascaded carry chain for the FIFO counters that uses much less logic than the default full carry-lookahead circuitry. The resulting FIFO compiles a lot faster, uses a lot less circuitry, but runs slower.
