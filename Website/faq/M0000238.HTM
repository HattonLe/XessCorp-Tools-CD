Appropriate pins for configuring an FPGA using XSLOAD

Q:

I just built an XCS10 board of my own that has the same connection to the PC Parallel port as the XS40. I download the same code that works on the XS40, and wham! The XCS10 never jumps out of config mode, as evidenced by the DONE, /LDC, and HDC pins.

Possibilities: I screwed up the mode control, or some other lines. I took my wiring from the XS40 v1.3 schematic, and so far everything has checked out. I have a scope comparing the two cases. The only difference so far is on the "Don't connect/M1" line, pin 30. I didn't connect anything to it, like the Spartan data sheet says. The XS40, with J10 removed, connects it to MODE and /PWRDWN through some resistors.

I can see the /PROGRAM (pin 55), TCK (pin 16), and TDI (pin 15) pins jump appropriately on both the working and non-working boards. Even /INIT (pin 41) behaves the same, bouncing low for 1.4ms at the beginning of the programming cycle.

It does seem funny to use /PROGRAM instead of TMS, in conjunction with TCK and TDI. Could this be an undocumented feature of the Spartan, and a relic from the XC4000 chips, that broke with a Spartan mask revision? Should I mess with xsload (I'm using an obsolete version, hacked for Linux) to make it hit TMS instead of /PROGRAM?

A:

Observe in the schematic that TDI (pin 15) and DIN (pin 71) are both connected to the parallel port pin which sources the configuration bits. Observe also that TCK (pin 16) and CCLK (pin 73) are both connected to the configuration clock sourced by the parallel port. The DIN and the CCLK pins are the important ones; the TDI and TCK pins were connected in case we wanted to configure the FPGA via JTAG, but that never happened. If you haven't connected DIN and CCLK, then your chip will never configure using XSLOAD because that's where the bits actually go into the chip.
