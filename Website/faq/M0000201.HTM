8031 runs at 100 MHz?!

Q:

I have a question regarding the speed which the 8031 is running at. This XS board has a Winbond 8031. I measured the frequency at XTAL1 and at ALE. I also wrote a short program to toggle P1.0 and measured the frequency at P1.0.

  • Frequency measured at XTAL1 is 100 MHz. 
  • Frequency measured at ALE is 16.67 MHz. 

From the frequency measured at P1.0, I calculate a machine cycle of 120 ns.  In case your are wondering, the program is

LOOP:   CPL     P1.0    (cycle: 1)
        JMP     LOOP    (cycle: 2)

This produces a square wave output at P1.0, whose period is 6 cycles.

They all indicate that the 8031 is indeed running at 100MHz. (ALE is always 1/6 of the system clock. One 8051 machine cycle = 12 ticks. Thus 1 tick = 10 ns, which corresponds to 100 MHz.) I am a newbie in 8031, but in my impression the Scenix chip is the only 8051 variant that runs at 100 MHz. (And they made a big deal out of it.) Does the above measurements mean I can plan my project with the assumption that the 8031 on the XS board can run at 100 MHz? I couldn't find Winbond's web site on the internet and therefore couldn't get to their data sheet. I did find that the 8031 sourced by Oki has a max speed of 24 MHz.

A:

The Winbond 8031 is rated at 24 MHz. The fact that yours is running at 100 MHz is no guarantee that it will run reliably. You will have to reprogram the oscillator for a lower speed or explicitly generate a slower clock for the 8031 using a clock divider in the FPGA.
