XS Board LED driven by leakage through VGA circuit

Q:

I am lighting more than two led segment when only one is told to light. These lines are shared with the VGA connector and are terminated with the 330 and 680 ohm resistor networks causing a current flow to the wrong Led segment. Is there a fix for this hardware problem???

A:

Yes, one LED segment can be weakly driven by the leakage through the VGA circuitry. We have not had any cases where the leakage-driven LED shines at close to the same magnitude as an actively-driven LED segment. Here are a couple of things you can do to lessen the problem:

  1. Make sure that the FPGA or CPLD I/O pin that is connected to the weakly-driven LED segment is actively driven to a logic 0 (low-voltage) state. This will drain off the leakage current before it can get to the LED segment. If you leave it in a high-impedance state (which is what will happen if you don't use the pin in your design), the leakage current can travel through the LED segment.
  2. Get a high-density DB15 plug and solder the red, green, and blue pins to the ground pin. Plug it into the high-density DB15 VGA socket on the XS Board. This will drain the leakage current before it can reach the LED segment.
  3. Use the LED digits on the XStend Board instead of the LED digit on the XS Board.
