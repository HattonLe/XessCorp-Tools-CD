Verifying XS Board designs

Q:

Once I verified the design via simulation, how can I determine that it works after I download it? Do I monitor it thru the parallel port? One way would be to output key signals to unused I/O pins. But it seems that most pins are in use.

A:

You can force some inputs through the data pins of the parallel port and read some of the pins back through the parallel port status pins. Or display results through the LED digit. Or monitor pins using a logic probe or oscilloscope.

If you hold the microcontroller in the reset state and raise the chip-enable of the RAM, then you can use all the FPGA I/O pins that are attached to these chips as general-purpose I/O. Check the XS40 programmer's model at http://www.xess.com/prgmdl40.pdf for a high-level view of the circuitry.

Q:

It's not clear to me that this can be done after the FPGA is downloaded, since the FPGA will lose it's data after a reset.

A:

The FPGA controls the reset pin of the microcontroller and the chip-enable, write-enable, and output-enable of the RAM chip. The FPGA cannot affect its own configuration when it drives these pins. Only the parallel port control outputs that are attached to the configuration pins of the FPGA can affect its configuration.
